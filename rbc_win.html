RCB 2025 win

The 2025 season was the 18th season of the Indian Premier League (IPL) for Royal Challengers Bengaluru (RCB). They were one of the ten teams participating in the tournament. Before the season started, Rajat Patidar was named as the captain, and Andy Flower was the head coach of the team.

RCB performed very well during the season and finished in second place in the league stage. They qualified for Qualifier 1 in the playoffs and made history by becoming the first IPL team to win all their away matches in a single season. In Qualifier 1, they defeated Punjab Kings and reached their fourth IPL final. In the final match, they once again defeated Punjab Kings and finally won their first IPL title after 18 years.

Virat Kohli was the top run scorer for RCB with 657 runs, while Josh Hazlewood was the leading wicket-taker with 22 wickets in the 2025 season.

Before the season, the IPL 2025 was announced as the 18th edition of the tournament, organized by the Board of Control for Cricket in India (BCCI). RCB was among the few teams that had not won an IPL title before this season. In the previous season, they had finished in fourth place.

The 2025 IPL season included ten teams and a total of 74 matches, played from 22 March to 3 June across 13 venues in India. RCB played most of their home matches at the M. Chinnaswamy Stadium. However, their last home match was shifted to Ekana Cricket Stadium due to weather conditions in Bengaluru.
